General Questions

What is this site about?

This site provides a leaderboard, tournament results and stats and graphs for our table tennis games.

Who updates the site?

The site is manually updated after matches by Aaro when they have the time.

Technical Questions

How do ratings work?

Ratings are based on the Glicko-1 rating system, by Mark Glickman. In our system, a new player receives 1500 as their rating and 350 as their RD.

What does RD mean?

RD stands for "rating deviation", and it is the numerical value of uncertainty around a player's rating. Higher RD, more uncertainty.

How is RD increase calculated?

You can find the formula in the Wikipedia page for the Glicko rating system. In our system t = amount of days since last played/recalculated RD, and a player returns to RD = 350 after 365 days of inactivity. A 'typical players' RD' is calculated by taking the median of all player RD's, with values of 175.0 or above disregarded.

Tournament strength? What is it?

Tournament strength is a rating value that tries to determine how difficult any given tournament would be to win. Without going into too much detail, this is achieved by calculating the probability of a player with 1500 rating winning the whole tournament. Using this probability value, we solve an equation to equate winning the whole tournament to winning a single high rated player. This imaginary high rated player's rating is the tournament strength.

How is peak/worst rating calculated?

Peak and worst ratings are calculated with a game-to-game basis.

How is peak/worst ranking calculated?

Peak and worst ratings are calculated with a tournament to tournament basis. A new player's ranking with the rating of 1500 is disregarded.
